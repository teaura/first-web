HELP END HUMAN TRAFFICKING


First Step To End Human Trafficking Is To Know What It Is And How It Happens

Human Trafficking involves the use of force, fraud, or coercion to obtain some type of labour or commercial sex act. Every year, millions of men, woman, and children are trafficked worlwide - including right here in South Africa. It happens in any community and victims are from all ages, race, gender, and nationality. Manipulation, violence, or false promises of well-paying jobs or even romantic relationships are used by traffickers to lure their victims into trafficking situations.

Traffickers often look fro people who are susceptible for a variety of reasons, including psychological or emotional vulnerability, economic hardship, lack of a social safety net, natural disasters, or political instability. The trauma caused by the traffickers can be so great that many may not identify themselves as victims or even ask for help, even in highly public settings.

Top 5 Things You Need To Know About Victim Identification Operations:

  1. Operations aim to help victims out of exploitation.
  2. Screens for potential victims that may be susceptible to trafficking.
  3. Primary goal is to provide the highest level of care to vulnerable individuals.
  4. The ops are proactive, working closely with local hotlines and law enforcement to collect data to build cases.
  5. It's a collaborative effort, often alongside local law enforcement, partners, and NGO's.
The key to identifying and safeguarding more victims is partnership and collaboration and our primary goal is to identify and intervene - providing the highest level of care for victims and potential victims of human trafficking.
